# HIP.markets Business And Product Spec

## Executive Summary

HIP.markets is a HyperEVM protocol and HIP-3 market operator. The protocol pools HYPE from users so the HIP.markets team can satisfy the 500,000 HYPE stake requirement to operate its own Hyperliquid HIP-3 builder-deployed perpetual DEX. In return, HYPE stakers receive a share of deployer trading fees generated by HIP.markets-operated markets.

The product is not simple liquid staking. It is a community-backed exchange-operator model. Depositors are backing the HIP.markets team's ability to launch markets, run oracle infrastructure, attract liquidity, manage risk, and share fees transparently.

## Core Thesis

HIP-3 turns market creation into an operator business. Builders can deploy perp DEXs on HyperCore, configure markets, run oracles, and receive deployer fees. The 500,000 HYPE stake requirement is a capital bottleneck. HIP.markets converts that bottleneck into a community-backed model: HYPE holders provide the deployer stake, the HIP.markets team operates the DEX, and stakers share in net builder fees.

## Judge-Ready Market Context

HIP.markets should be explained from Hyperliquid's strength, not from generic DeFi yield. DefiLlama's Hyperliquid perp reference showed roughly $164.2B in 30-day perp volume, $1.33B in 24-hour perp volume, $7.3B in open interest, and $701M in annualized revenue during the 2026-05-17 research pass. HIP-3 builders are therefore not launching into a cold-start venue; they are building on top of proven exchange-scale infrastructure.

The bottleneck is the operator stake. Hyperliquid's HIP-3 docs specify a 500,000 HYPE mainnet stake requirement per perp DEX. At the 2026-05-17 Hyperliquid API HYPE price check of $42.848, that stake is about $21.42M before oracle, data, market-maker, legal, audit, and incident-response costs. For many operators this is too capital-intensive; for most HYPE holders it is too large to access directly.

The incentive gap is equally important. Trade.xyz demonstrates that HIP-3 can attract real demand, but the standard operator model routes deployer fees to the operator fee recipient. Ordinary HYPE staking references are around 2.37% APY, so HIP.markets creates a distinct higher-risk product category: HYPE holders can underwrite a market operator and receive transparent exposure to net deployer fees from markets they helped fund.

## Product

### MVP

- One HIP.markets-operated HIP-3 DEX.
- One HYPE vault on HyperEVM.
- One initial market category with up to three initial tickers.
- Conservative deposit cap.
- Weekly reward distributions.
- Public dashboard for stake, fees, oracle health, market volume, and risk state.
- No third-party operator onboarding.

### User Flow

1. User deposits HYPE into the HIP.markets vault.
2. User receives vault shares.
3. Vault HYPE is allocated toward the HIP.markets HIP-3 deployer stake.
4. Once the vault reaches the 500,000 HYPE requirement and the risk checklist is complete, the operator multisig escrows stake to the deployer stake controller.
5. HIP.markets records HIP-3 operator approval and launches markets.
6. Fees accrue to the HIP.markets fee recipient.
7. Net fees are distributed to vault stakers after operating fees, protocol fees, and reserve contributions.
8. Users request withdrawal through a queue that respects HIP-3 stake and market shutdown constraints.

### Operator Lifecycle

The product should make lifecycle state visible instead of hiding it behind an APR number:

| Phase | Meaning | User impact |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Funding | Vault has not reached 500,000 HYPE | Deposits open, stake not yet submitted |
| Stake Ready | Vault has enough HYPE, checklist still being finalized | Deposits may be capped, operator preparing stake submission |
| Stake Escrowed | HYPE has moved to the deployer stake controller | Withdrawals become more constrained |
| Operator Approved | HIP.markets has slashable HIP-3 operator status | Market launch can proceed |
| Markets Live | Markets are trading and generating deployer fees | Rewards can accrue |
| Wind Down | Deposits paused, stake/markets being unwound | Withdrawals handled through queue/liquidity |
| Slashed | Stake loss recorded | Principal can be impaired |

## Key Personas

Passive HYPE holder: Wants exposure to builder fee revenue without operating markets.

Trader: Wants new Hyperliquid markets with transparent risk and oracle methodology.

Market maker: Wants new venues, incentive programs, and clear market parameters.

Data/oracle provider: Sells feeds, relayer services, and monitoring infrastructure.

Risk committee: Approves market launches, caps, reserves, and incident responses.

## Cost Stack

The 500,000 HYPE stake is only the entry requirement. HIP.markets also needs:

- Additional ticker auction budget after the first three markets.
- Oracle relayers expected to update roughly every three seconds.
- Licensed and reliable data feeds.
- Market-maker incentives.
- Frontend, indexers, analytics, and monitoring.
- Key management and multisig operations.
- Legal, compliance, and jurisdiction controls.
- Smart contract audits.
- Slashing reserve and incident response budget.

## Market Launch Strategy

HIP.markets should not begin with the most exotic markets. The first three markets should be:

- liquid enough to attract market makers;
- simple enough to source reliable oracle data;
- defensible enough to build a brand;
- low enough risk to avoid early slashing or oracle incidents.

Recommended launch candidates:

- crypto majors with strong data availability;
- highly liquid RWA assets with strong Pyth or institutional feeds;
- one thematic index only if methodology and data licensing are clear.

## Economic Model

Gross trading fees = daily volume * effective fee rate

Builder revenue = gross trading fees * deployer share

Net distributable revenue = builder revenue - operating fee - protocol fee - reserve contribution

Staker APR = net distributable daily revenue * 365 / value of staked HYPE

Important sensitivities:

- Growth mode can materially reduce fees.
- Deployer fee scale can make two DEXs with the same volume produce very different APRs.
- Low oracle or liquidity quality can destroy the business through slashing risk.
- High volume is not enough if operating costs and reserves consume fee revenue.

## Governance

MVP governance should be a risk committee plus multisig. Broad token governance should come later.

Responsibilities:

- approve market categories;
- set initial tickers;
- set vault caps;
- approve oracle methodology;
- approve operating budget;
- set fee-sharing terms;
- pause deposits during incidents;
- halt or sunset markets;
- publish monthly risk and revenue reports.

## Roadmap

### V1

- HIP.markets HYPE vault.
- One HIP-3 DEX.
- Three initial markets.
- Weekly distributions.
- Manual/semi-automated accounting.
- Public dashboard.

### V2

- Multiple HIP.markets market vaults.
- Automated fee verification.
- Transfer-restricted receipt token.
- Insurance reserve vault.
- Better oracle monitoring.
- Market-maker incentive dashboard.

### V3

- Risk tranching.
- Secondary market for vault shares.
- HIP-4 outcome markets if mechanics mature.
- Third-party operator financing only after the first-party model is proven.

## Positioning

HIP.markets is the community-backed HIP-3 exchange operator.

Compared with trade.xyz and HyENA, HIP.markets differentiates by letting HYPE holders directly back the operator stake and share in net deployer fees. Compared with liquid staking, yield comes from market operation rather than only validator rewards. Compared with launchpads, HIP.markets provides ongoing stake, monitoring, and fee-sharing for a live market operator.

## Legal Notes

HIP.markets may create securities, derivatives, commodities, market-data, and jurisdiction risks. The MVP should assume:

- no fixed yield claims;
- explicit slashing-risk disclosure;
- possible geo-restrictions;
- legal entity structuring;
- no U.S. user access without counsel approval;
- audited contracts before deposits.

## Bottom Line

HIP.markets only works if the team operates markets safely and profitably. The vault is the capital layer, but the business is market operations: oracle reliability, liquidity, risk management, fee accounting, and trust.
